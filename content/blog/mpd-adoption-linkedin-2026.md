# LinkedIn Post — MPD Adoption on Drillships

---

MPD adoption on drillships is following the Cyberbase curve — and that curve has a known shape.

Cyberbase took roughly 8 years from niche capability to new-build standard. MPD deepwater adoption
started in earnest around 2017–2018. Do the math.

The engineering case is the reservoir, not the technology.

Sub-salt. Lower Tertiary. HPHT. These wells have pressure windows you cannot manage conventionally —
sometimes less than 0.5 ppg between pore pressure and fracture gradient. That connection ECD swing
that was manageable in a conventional well? In a 0.3 ppg window at 25,000 ft TVD, it takes you
outside the envelope in both directions simultaneously. There is no mud weight that solves that
problem. CBHP does.

Fleet data from our worldenergydata rig tracking:

- Drillship MPD penetration: ~66% of active fleet as of early 2026
- New 7th-gen builds: arriving MPD-integrated as standard
- Transocean Atlas and Titan: 20K BOP + AFGlobal DAPC from day one
- Operators in the GOM Lower Tertiary play: MPD is now a mandatory tender requirement, not a preference

The contractors who moved early are accumulating something harder to replicate than the equipment
itself — crew experience. A team with 20 MPD wells thinks differently than a team on well number two.
That gap compounds.

For drilling engineers: MPD fluency is becoming core competency for deepwater work, not a specialization.
The wellbore pressure management foundation is the same. The mental model — from reactive to anticipatory —
is the shift.
