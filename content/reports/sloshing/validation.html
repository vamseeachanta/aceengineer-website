Tank Sloshing CFD Validation

A fixed review point for subject-matter experts. Every number below comes from the pinned public release; report pages should link here when they claim analytical agreement, convergence evidence, or numerical qualification.

Engineering disposition

The published solver reproduces the rectangular-tank first-mode frequency within 0.2964–0.3264% across seven accepted free-decay cases. The stationary baseline, refined forced-roll sweep, complete-cycle fields, and sectional reconstruction support comparative screening within the published model and loading envelope. This is verification evidence, not full vessel validation.

Review checks and acceptance meaning

Analytical agreement

Measured free-decay frequencies span 0.755580–0.755807 Hz against the 0.758054 Hz linear-wave target. All seven metric records are marked pass.

Timestep sensitivity

The 0.001 s and 0.004 s cases return 0.755717 Hz and 0.755702 Hz. This supports timestep stability for the measured frequency in this benchmark.

Mesh evidence

Five free-decay meshes from 1,600 to 25,600 cells remain close to the analytical target. The release deliberately classifies the convergence study as non_asymptotic_caution.

Forced-roll confirmation

The 20,480-cell and 69,120-cell T = 24 s cases provide a two-level mesh comparison. The published exchange-amplitude change is 0.783862%.

Numerical caveat retained and reduced

The original pressure follow-on configured a maximum Courant number of 0.35 but observed 0.47841479. The new fine rerun tightened the target to 0.25 and reduced the observed maximum to 0.32648391, with 0.00280% volume drift and final-cycle changes below 0.08%. It completed and converged, but still exceeded its configured target; both records therefore retain publish_with_courant_caveat.

What this fixture does not validate

It does not establish full-scale vessel behavior, structural stress, air-entrainment fidelity, local impact-pressure convergence, response at other fill levels or amplitudes, or anti-roll performance. Those require purpose-specific experimental or field benchmarks and additional numerical qualification.
