Methodology

1. Overview

The HSE Risk Index quantifies occupational and environmental risk using a three-dimensional framework:

  • Acute Risk — sudden incident severity (fatalities, injuries, spills) weighted at 50%.
  • Chronic Risk — long-term exposure proxied by EPA Toxic Release Inventory carcinogen emissions, weighted at 25%.
  • Compliance Risk — regulatory non-compliance rate from BSEE inspection records, weighted at 25%.

2. Composite Formula

Composite Score = 0.50 × Acute + 0.25 × Chronic + 0.25 × Compliance

Within the Acute dimension, sub-weights are: Fatality rate (0.40), Injury rate (0.35), Weighted incident count (0.25).

3. Normalization

Raw metrics are converted to percentile ranks within the dataset and mapped to a 1–10 scale. A score of 1 represents the lowest relative risk; 10 represents the highest within the assessed activity set.

6. Caveats and Limitations

  • Relative, not absolute. Scores indicate ranking within this dataset, not the probability of an incident occurring.
  • Source coverage varies. BSEE covers offshore operations; OSHA covers onshore workplaces. Cross-domain comparisons should be made with care.
  • Chronic dimension is a proxy. EPA TRI captures chemical releases but does not directly measure worker exposure or health outcomes.
  • Low-confidence flag. Activities with fewer than 10 total incidents are marked low confidence. Interpret these scores with caution.
  • Water depth bands. Shallow <500 ft, Mid 500–5,000 ft, Deep >5,000 ft. Activities with "all" band aggregate across depths.
