AceEngineer Recommendations

•Define the running of a dairy farm with the following factors:
•Reliable working employees with no automation of feed or milking.
•Limited Investment from trusted investors is initial considered to maintain independence of operations.
•Grouped investment will be considered at a later states.
•Distribution of profits in proportional and ethical manner.
•All working employees will be compensated with salary for any day to day work done.
•Extend this business analysis from 10 year to 20 years of business running period to understand long-term trends.
•Implement automation at a livestock numbers of 30-40 and keep the work force number fixed.
•If dairy farm is started in remote area where continuous trusted monitoring is not possible, we should have an automated/ online monitoring system installed;
•We should record the every movement in dairy farm and track it. This monitoring will be conducted by one trust worth person regularly (3-5 times/week);
•Importantly farm should be expandable for particular time slice. (ex: we start with 10 LS, after two years the farm should contain > 10LS);
•Conduct milk processing industry analysis once smooth running of dairy farm is achieved.

Analysis Approach

Here we explain the Analysis of Dairy Farm developed by AceEngineer.
The Major things in Analysis Approach are
• Location of Farm and Investment Year
• Farm Size( Start with No of Females & Males)
• Milk Production System

• Overview of Dairy Farm Plan:



•Total Live Stock System


 
• The Diagram explains about Total Live Stock System
• Our Basic Assumption is Every 2 years is average Calving period.
• Successfully birth ratio is 1%
• Male and Female Ratio is 0.5%
• After 3 years newly born females become a Gestating Live stock
• Keep required males and sell unwanted male live stocks
• Repeat this procedure for every year




•Total Milk Production System



• The Diagram explains about Total Milk Production System.
• Our Basic Assumption is Each Live Stock Produce 6 liters of milk per day
• and Each newly born live stock consumed 1.5 liters of milk per day
• At finally we get 4.5 liters of milk from each live stock
• Those milk is available for Sold
•Fixed Investment includes Live Stock Cost, Land Rate and Equipments
• Variable Cost includes Feed Rate, Workers Wages, Power Connection, Pump Connection, cell bill, Insurance bill, Maintenance cost
     Manager Salary, Vet Doctor Fees, Medicine Fees and others.
• Every year we include 10% maintenance Cost Expenses
• Expenses = Fixed Cost + Variable Cost
